🌹 Netflix Version - Episode 3: "The Balcony"

Scene: 8-minute sequence with Romeo's internal monologue, flashbacks to his day, Juliet's relationship with her nurse, family tension buildup. Soft lighting, multiple camera angles, background music swells.

Audience Experience: Deep emotional investment, character development, binge-worthy cliffhanger.

💕 TikTok Version - "POV: Balcony Scene"

Scene: 60 seconds, trending audio, quick cuts between Romeo and Juliet, text overlays for dialogue, perfect ring lighting. Comments: "NOT THEM FALLING IN LOVE 💀" "Part 2???"

Audience Experience: Instant gratification, shareability, part of a larger trend.

🎙️ Podcast Version - "Voices in the Night"

Scene: Intimate whispers, footsteps on stone, night sounds, voice acting carries all emotion. No visuals - your imagination creates the perfect Romeo and Juliet.

Audience Experience: Personal, immersive, creates mental movies.

🎨 Short Film Version - "One Perfect Moment"

Scene: Cinematographic masterpiece, every shot composed like art, minimal dialogue, visual storytelling, golden hour lighting, symbolic imagery.

Audience Experience: Aesthetic inspiration, artistic appreciation, festival-worthy.

🎯 Your Challenge

Pick any story you love (movie, book, personal experience) and imagine how it would work on three different platforms.

Questions to ask:

  • What would you keep? 🤔
  • What would you change? ✨
